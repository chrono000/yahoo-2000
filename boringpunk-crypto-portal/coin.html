Bitcoin (BTC) is the original and largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization. Created in 2009 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto, it operates on a decentralized proof-of-work blockchain. See also Ethereum (ETH) for comparison with the leading smart contract platform and Solana (SOL) for a high-throughput alternative.

⭐ Key Insight
"Digital gold thesis." Bitcoin's fixed supply of 21 million coins and its halving schedule (block reward cuts every ~4 years) create a predictable, deflationary monetary policy — unique among financial assets.
